Frequently Asked Questions

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is the fixed amount you pay to your lender every month towards loan repayment. EMI includes both principal and interest components.

EMI is calculated using formula: EMI = [P × R × (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N − 1], where P is principal, R is monthly interest rate, and N is loan tenure in months.

Three main factors affect EMI: Loan amount (Principal), Interest rate, and Loan tenure. Higher principal or rate increases EMI; longer tenure decreases EMI but increases total interest.
