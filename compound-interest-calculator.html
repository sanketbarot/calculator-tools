About Compound Interest Calculator

Compound Interest is the 8th wonder of the world as Albert Einstein famously said. It is the most powerful concept in finance that helps your money grow exponentially over time. Use this calculator to see how your investments can multiply with compounding.

📐 Compound Interest Formula

A = P × (1 + r/n)^(n × t)

Where:

  • A = Final Amount (Principal + Interest)
  • P = Principal amount (initial investment)
  • r = Annual interest rate (as decimal)
  • n = Number of times interest compounds per year
  • t = Time period in years

Compound Interest = A − P

🎯 What is Compound Interest?

Compound interest is calculated on both the initial principal AND the accumulated interest from previous periods. Unlike simple interest, your interest earns more interest over time, creating exponential growth.

✅ How to Use This Calculator

  1. Enter your Principal Amount (initial investment)
  2. Enter the Annual Interest Rate (%)
  3. Select Time Period in years or months
  4. Choose Compounding Frequency (more frequent = more returns)
  5. Click Calculate to see final amount & interest earned

💡 Power of Compounding (Examples)

Let's see how ₹1,00,000 grows at 10% annual interest with different time periods:

  • After 5 years: ₹1,63,862 (Interest: ₹63,862)
  • After 10 years: ₹2,70,704 (Interest: ₹1,70,704)
  • After 15 years: ₹4,47,200 (Interest: ₹3,47,200)
  • After 20 years: ₹7,38,873 (Interest: ₹6,38,873)
  • After 30 years: ₹20,12,963 (Interest: ₹19,12,963)

Lesson: Longer the time, more powerful the compounding! Start early.

📊 Compounding Frequency Impact

For ₹1,00,000 @ 10% for 10 years:

  • Yearly: ₹2,59,374
  • Half-Yearly: ₹2,65,330
  • Quarterly: ₹2,68,506
  • Monthly: ₹2,70,704
  • Daily: ₹2,71,791

More frequent compounding = Higher returns!

🚀 Where Compound Interest Applies

  • Savings Accounts — Daily/Quarterly compounding
  • Fixed Deposits — Quarterly compounding
  • Mutual Funds — Daily NAV growth
  • Stock Market — Annual returns reinvested
  • PPF (Public Provident Fund) — Yearly compounding
  • Bonds & Debentures — Semi-annual compounding
  • Credit Cards — Monthly compounding (negative side!)

⚠️ Simple vs Compound Interest

If you invest ₹1,00,000 @ 10% for 10 years:

  • Simple Interest: Earn ₹1,00,000 (Total: ₹2,00,000)
  • Compound Interest: Earn ₹1,70,704 (Total: ₹2,70,704)

Compound interest gives you ₹70,704 extra in 10 years!

Frequently Asked Questions

Compound interest is interest calculated on the initial principal as well as accumulated interest from previous periods. It makes your money grow exponentially because you earn "interest on interest".

Compound Interest formula: A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt), where P is principal, r is annual rate, n is compounding frequency per year, and t is time in years. Then CI = A − P.

Compound interest is better for investments as you earn more over time. Simple interest is better for loans as you pay less total interest. Always check which one applies before signing!

Daily compounding gives the highest returns, followed by monthly, quarterly, half-yearly, and yearly. However, the difference is small for short tenures. Continuous compounding gives slightly more than daily.

The Rule of 72 tells you how many years it takes to double your money with compound interest. Formula: 72 ÷ interest rate. Example: At 8% interest, money doubles in 72/8 = 9 years.

Yes, PPF (Public Provident Fund) uses annual compounding. Current PPF rate is around 7.1% per year. PPF is one of the best tax-free compound interest investments in India.
