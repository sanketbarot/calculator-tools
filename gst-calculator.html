Frequently Asked Questions

GST (Goods and Services Tax) is a unified indirect tax in India levied on the supply of goods and services. It replaced multiple cascading taxes like VAT, service tax, excise duty, etc. GST was introduced on 1st July 2017.

GST in India has 5 slab rates: 0%, 5%, 12%, 18%, and 28%. Most goods and services fall under 18% slab. Luxury and sin goods attract 28% plus cess.

CGST (Central GST) and SGST (State GST) apply equally for intra-state sales. IGST (Integrated GST) applies for inter-state sales. Formula: CGST + SGST = IGST.

To remove GST from inclusive price, use: Original = Inclusive × (100 / (100 + GST%)). Example: ₹1,180 with 18% GST → Original = 1180 × (100/118) = ₹1,000. GST = ₹180.
