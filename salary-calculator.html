About Salary Calculator

Confused why your in-hand salary is much less than your CTC? Our Salary Calculator breaks down your CTC into Gross Salary, deductions (PF, tax, professional tax), and final Take-Home Pay. It supports both Old and New tax regimes, perfect for Indian employees and HR professionals.

📐 Salary Calculation Formula

Gross Salary = CTC − (Employer PF + Gratuity + Bonus)
Net Salary = Gross Salary − (Employee PF + Tax + PT)

💰 Common Salary Components

Earnings (What You Get):

  • Basic Salary: 40-50% of CTC (fully taxable)
  • HRA (House Rent Allowance): 40% (non-metro) or 50% (metro) of Basic
  • Special Allowance: Remaining after Basic + HRA
  • DA (Dearness Allowance): Common in government jobs
  • LTA (Leave Travel Allowance): Travel expense exemption
  • Medical Allowance: Up to ₹15,000/year
  • Conveyance Allowance: Up to ₹1,600/month
  • Performance Bonus: Variable based on performance

Deductions (What's Cut):

  • Employee PF: 12% of Basic salary
  • Professional Tax: ₹150-200/month (varies by state)
  • Income Tax (TDS): As per tax slab
  • Insurance: Health, life premiums
  • Loan EMI: If salary advance taken

💡 Tips to Increase Take-Home Salary

  • Use Section 80C deductions — PPF, ELSS, LIC (₹1.5L limit)
  • Health Insurance (80D) — Save up to ₹25,000-50,000
  • HRA exemption — Submit rent receipts
  • NPS contribution (80CCD) — Extra ₹50,000 deduction
  • LTA claims — Travel exemption
  • Home loan interest — Up to ₹2L deduction
  • Education loan interest — Full deduction
  • Choose right regime — Compare both, pick lower tax

🆚 Old vs New Regime: Which to Choose?

  • Choose New Regime if: Few deductions, salary 5-15 LPA, want simplicity
  • Choose Old Regime if: Heavy deductions (80C, HRA, home loan), salary above 15 LPA
  • Calculate both to see which saves more tax

⚠️ Common Misconceptions

  • CTC ≠ In-Hand Salary — Difference can be 20-30%
  • Employer PF contribution is part of CTC but doesn't reach you
  • Gratuity is part of CTC but paid only on 5+ years tenure
  • Bonus is variable — Don't assume guaranteed
  • Stock options (ESOPs) — Future value, not immediate

🏙️ State-wise Professional Tax

  • Maharashtra: ₹200/month (max ₹2,500/year)
  • Karnataka: ₹200/month
  • West Bengal: ₹110-200/month
  • Andhra Pradesh: ₹150-200/month
  • Tamil Nadu: ₹208/month
  • Delhi, UP, Haryana: No professional tax

Frequently Asked Questions

CTC (Cost to Company) is the total amount an employer spends on an employee per year, including salary, allowances, employer PF, gratuity, and benefits. In-hand salary is 20-30% less than CTC due to deductions.

In-Hand Salary = Gross Salary − (PF + Professional Tax + Income Tax + Other Deductions). Gross Salary = CTC − (Employer PF + Gratuity + Bonus). Our calculator does all this automatically.

Gross Salary is total earnings before deductions. Net Salary (In-Hand) is what you actually receive after deducting PF, tax, and other deductions from Gross.

New Regime is better for those with few deductions and salary 5-15 LPA. Old Regime is better if you can claim heavy deductions (80C, HRA, home loan interest). Always calculate both to compare.

12% of your Basic Salary is deducted as Employee PF. Employer also contributes 12% (part of your CTC). Total 24% goes to your EPF account, earning interest tax-free.

HRA Exemption (under Old Regime) is the least of: (a) Actual HRA received, (b) 50% of Basic (metro) / 40% (non-metro), (c) Rent paid − 10% of Basic. Submit rent receipts to claim.

Yes, bonus is usually included in CTC but it's variable. Performance bonus, joining bonus, retention bonus may or may not be paid based on conditions. Always confirm with HR.
