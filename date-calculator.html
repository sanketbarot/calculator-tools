About Date Calculator

Our Date Calculator is a powerful tool for any date-related calculation. Whether you need to find the difference between two dates, add days to a deadline, calculate working days for a project, or plan an event — this calculator handles it all with one click.

🎯 Two Powerful Modes

1️⃣ Date Difference Mode

Find the exact difference between any two dates in:

  • Years, Months, Days — Calendar breakdown
  • Total Days — Exact day count
  • Total Weeks — Week count
  • Working Days — Business days (Mon-Fri)
  • Weekend Days — Saturdays + Sundays
  • Total Hours — Hour calculation

2️⃣ Add / Subtract Mode

Calculate a new date by adding or subtracting from any starting date:

  • Add/Subtract Years
  • Add/Subtract Months
  • Add/Subtract Weeks
  • Add/Subtract Days
  • Combine multiple units in single calculation

✅ How to Use This Calculator

For Date Difference:

  1. Select Date Difference mode
  2. Enter From Date and To Date
  3. Optionally check "Include End Date"
  4. Click Calculate to see complete breakdown

For Add/Subtract:

  1. Select Add / Subtract mode
  2. Enter Starting Date
  3. Choose Add or Subtract
  4. Enter years, months, weeks, days
  5. Click Calculate to see resulting date

📊 Important Date Facts

  • 1 Year = 365 days (366 in leap years)
  • 1 Month = 28, 29, 30, or 31 days
  • 1 Week = 7 days
  • 1 Day = 24 hours = 1,440 minutes = 86,400 seconds
  • Leap Year = Year divisible by 4 (except centuries not divisible by 400)
  • Working Days in Year = ~260 days (excluding weekends)
  • Weekend Days in Year = ~104-105 days

🗓️ Leap Year Rules

  • Year is divisible by 4 → Leap Year
  • BUT year is divisible by 100 → NOT a Leap Year
  • UNLESS year is divisible by 400 → Leap Year (e.g., 2000)

Examples: 2020, 2024, 2028 are leap years. 1900 was NOT, but 2000 WAS.

📐 How Date Calculations Work

Our calculator uses JavaScript's Date object which accurately handles:

  • Varying month lengths (28-31 days)
  • Leap years (Feb 29)
  • Daylight Saving Time transitions
  • Time zones (local browser time)
  • Calendar transitions

🌏 Calendar Systems

Our calculator uses the Gregorian Calendar, the most widely used calendar system globally. Other calendars include:

  • Hindu/Vikram Samvat — Used in India for religious dates
  • Islamic/Hijri — Used in Islamic countries
  • Hebrew — Used by Jewish community
  • Chinese Lunar — Used for Chinese New Year
  • Buddhist — Used in Thailand, Cambodia

⚠️ Tips & Best Practices

  • Always double-check dates before important calculations
  • Consider time zones for international calculations
  • Account for public holidays in working day calculations
  • Use "Include End Date" option for inclusive counts
  • For pregnancy/medical use, consult professionals too
