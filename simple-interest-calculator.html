About Simple Interest Calculator

Simple Interest (SI) is the most basic way to calculate interest on a loan or investment. It is calculated only on the original principal amount throughout the entire period, without considering accumulated interest. Our calculator helps students, borrowers, and lenders quickly calculate SI using the standard P × R × T formula.

📐 Simple Interest Formula

SI = (P × R × T) / 100

Where:

  • SI = Simple Interest
  • P = Principal (initial loan or investment)
  • R = Rate of Interest per year (%)
  • T = Time period in years

Total Amount (A) = P + SI

🎯 What is Simple Interest?

Simple Interest is the interest calculated only on the original principal, not on the accumulated interest. Unlike compound interest, the interest amount remains the same every year. SI is straight-line interest with no compounding effect.

✅ How to Use This Calculator

  1. Enter the Principal Amount (P) in rupees
  2. Enter the Rate of Interest (R) per year as percentage
  3. Select Time (T) in years, months or days

💡 Example Calculations

Example 1: Principal = ₹50,000, Rate = 8%, Time = 5 years

  • SI = (50,000 × 8 × 5) / 100 = ₹20,000
  • Total Amount = 50,000 + 20,000 = ₹70,000

Example 2: Principal = ₹1,00,000, Rate = 10%, Time = 3 years

  • SI = (1,00,000 × 10 × 3) / 100 = ₹30,000
  • Total Amount = 1,00,000 + 30,000 = ₹1,30,000

🏦 Where Simple Interest Applies

  • Personal Loans — Many lenders calculate interest on a flat (simple) basis
  • Car Loans — Some auto loans use simple interest
  • Short-Term Loans — Bridge loans, payday loans
  • Treasury Bills — Government short-term securities
  • Bonds — Some bonds pay simple interest periodically
  • Education Loans — Interest during study period
  • Money Lending — Informal loans between individuals

⚠️ Simple vs Compound Interest

If you invest ₹1,00,000 @ 10% for 5 years:

  • Simple Interest: ₹50,000 (Total: ₹1,50,000)
  • Compound Interest: ₹61,051 (Total: ₹1,61,051)

Difference: ₹11,051 extra with compound interest. Higher the duration, bigger the difference!

📌 Key Points to Remember

  • Simple interest stays constant every year
  • Best for borrowers (less interest to pay)
  • Not ideal for long-term investments (no compounding)
  • Time can be in years, months, or days
  • Convert: 1 month = 1/12 year, 1 day = 1/365 year
