Frequently Asked Questions

SIP stands for Systematic Investment Plan. It is a method of investing a fixed amount in mutual funds at regular intervals (usually monthly). SIP helps build wealth over time through disciplined investing.

SIP returns are calculated using the formula: FV = P × ({[(1+r)^n - 1] / r}) × (1+r), where P is monthly investment, r is monthly rate, and n is total months.

Equity mutual funds typically deliver 10-15% annualized returns over the long term (10+ years). Debt funds give 6-8% returns. Past performance doesn't guarantee future results.

SIP is better for salaried investors as it averages out market volatility. Lumpsum can give higher returns when markets are at lows.
