Frequently Asked Questions

SIP stands for Systematic Investment Plan. It is a method of investing a fixed amount in mutual funds at regular intervals (usually monthly). SIP helps build wealth over time through disciplined investing and rupee cost averaging.

SIP returns are calculated using formula: FV = P × ({[(1+r)^n − 1] / r}) × (1+r), where P is monthly investment, r is monthly rate, and n is total months.

Equity mutual funds typically deliver 10–15% annualized returns over the long term (10+ years). Debt funds give 6–8% returns. Past performance doesn't guarantee future results.

Yes! Most mutual fund houses allow you to increase SIP amount anytime via Top-up SIP or step-up SIP option. You can also start additional SIPs in different funds.

SIP is better for salaried investors as it averages out market volatility. Lumpsum can give higher returns when markets are at lows. SIP is generally safer and more disciplined for long-term wealth building.

Yes, SIP is one of the safest ways to invest in mutual funds long-term. It reduces market timing risk and benefits from compounding. However, all mutual fund investments are subject to market risks.
